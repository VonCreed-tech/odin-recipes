Guyanese Fried Bakes

Description

Looking for the perfect Guyanese fried bake recipe? This soft, fluffy, and golden Caribbean bread is a breakfast favorite across Guyana and the Caribbean. Also known as float bake, it’s delicious served with saltfish, eggs, cheese, or just butter.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 2 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 4 tbsp butter, softened
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup warm water (or half water, half milk for a richer dough)

Steps

  1. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine flour, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Add butter: Use your hands or a spoon to rub the softened butter into the flour mixture until it resembles fine crumbs.
  3. Dissolve sugar: Mix the sugar into the warm water (or water/milk mixture), stirring until dissolved.
  4. Form the dough: Gradually add the sweetened liquid to the flour mixture, kneading for about 5 minutes until a smooth, soft dough forms.
  5. Let it rest: Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes. This helps create a light, fluffy texture when frying.
  6. Shape the bakes (choose your preferred method):

    Method 1: Roll out the dough to about ½ inch thickness and cut circles using a biscuit cutter.

    Method 2: Divide the dough into 6 equal pieces, shape each into a ball, and roll each one into a ½-inch thick circle. Lightly press the edges to prevent them from being too thick.

  7. Fry the bakes: In a deep pot or frying pan, heat about 3 inches of oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, carefully place a dough round into the oil. As it puffs up, gently spoon hot oil over the top to help it float and cook evenly.
  8. Flip and cook: Once golden brown on one side, flip and cook the other side until golden and crisp. Remove and drain on paper towels.
